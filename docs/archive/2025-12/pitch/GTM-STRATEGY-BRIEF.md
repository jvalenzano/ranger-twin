## Expert Panel Prompt

### Context for the Panel

You are a cross-functional advisory panel consisting of:

- **VC Partner** (Enterprise SaaS, GovTech focus) — Pattern recognition, market positioning, funding narratives
- **Startup CMO** (B2B/B2G experience) — Messaging, content strategy, launch sequencing
- **Federal Sales Executive** (20+ years USDA/DOI) — Procurement realities, stakeholder mapping, trust-building
- **Developer Advocate** (Open source, AI/ML tools) — Community building, technical credibility, GitHub presence
- **Personal Branding Consultant** (Tech executives) — Thought leadership, LinkedIn strategy, conference positioning

### The Opportunity

**RANGER** is an AI-powered coordination platform for post-fire forest recovery, built for the U.S. Forest Service. But it's actually three things:

1. **A Product** — Multi-agent AI system that compresses weeks of recovery analysis into hours
2. **A Pattern** — "Skills-First Architecture" that's replicable across any federal domain
3. **A Case Study** — Real-world implementation of cutting-edge AI (Google ADK, MCP, Gemini 2.0) in a federal context

The builder (Jason) is CTO at jvalenzano, a consultancy. He's building RANGER as a client engagement but also as:
- A proof-of-concept for jvalenzano's AI practice
- A personal portfolio piece demonstrating federal AI expertise
- An open-source project for community contribution and visibility

### Strategic Constraints

| Dimension | Reality |
|-----------|---------|
| **Budget** | Bootstrapped; no marketing spend beyond time |
| **Timeline** | Demo-ready Q1 2026; thought leadership can start now |
| **IP** | Open source on GitHub; no proprietary lock-in |
| **Audience Complexity** | Must work for GS-15s AND VCs AND developers |
| **Compliance** | Nothing classified; all public data; federal-friendly messaging |

### The Ask

Develop a comprehensive GTM strategy that addresses **five distinct audiences** with **coordinated but differentiated messaging**. For each audience, recommend:

1. **Primary narrative** — What story resonates?
2. **Content formats** — What medium works best?
3. **Distribution channels** — Where do they consume content?
4. **Proof points** — What evidence builds credibility?
5. **Call to action** — What do we want them to do?

## Audience Profiles

### Audience 1: jvalenzano Internal (Consultants, Leadership)

**Who:** Fellow consultants, practice leads, executive sponsors  
**Goal:** Evangelize Skills-First as a repeatable methodology; secure internal investment  
**Challenge:** Competing priorities; skepticism of "AI hype"  
**Success Metric:** Other engagements adopt the pattern; RANGER becomes a reference architecture

**Questions for the Panel:**
- How do we position this internally without overselling?
- What internal artifacts build credibility (lunch-and-learns, internal case studies)?
- How do we make the methodology teachable to other teams?

---

### Audience 2: Federal Service Executives (Forest Service, USDA)

**Who:** Forest Supervisors, Regional Foresters, CIOs, Program Managers  
**Goal:** Pilot adoption; prove ROI; expand to production  
**Challenge:** Risk aversion; procurement complexity; "we've seen AI demos before"  
**Success Metric:** 3 regional offices in pilot; positive feedback for expansion

**Questions for the Panel:**
- What's the trust-building sequence for federal buyers?
- How do we overcome "demo magic" skepticism?
- What proof points matter most (cost savings, time savings, compliance)?
- Should we lead with the problem or the solution?

---

### Audience 3: Adjacent Federal Agencies (NRCS, BLM, NPS, FEMA)

**Who:** Technical leaders at agencies with similar challenges (natural resource management, disaster recovery)  
**Goal:** Demonstrate pattern applicability; seed future engagements  
**Challenge:** They don't have fire recovery problems—they have *their own* problems  
**Success Metric:** Inbound interest; "could you build this for [our domain]?"

**Questions for the Panel:**
- How do we abstract RANGER into a reusable narrative?
- What's the "Skills-First for X" positioning?
- Should we create domain-agnostic materials or customize per agency?
- How do we avoid looking like we're selling vaporware?

---

### Audience 4: Open Source / Developer Community

**Who:** AI engineers, GovTech developers, Google ADK enthusiasts, MCP implementers  
**Goal:** GitHub stars, contributors, technical credibility  
**Challenge:** Crowded space; federal context may seem niche  
**Success Metric:** 500+ GitHub stars; 3+ external contributors; conference talk invitations

**Questions for the Panel:**
- What makes an open-source AI project "interesting" to developers?
- How do we position federal use case as an advantage, not a limitation?
- What content drives GitHub engagement (README quality, tutorials, architecture docs)?
- Should we extract reusable components (e.g., Proof Layer as standalone library)?
- How do we balance "polished product" vs. "work in progress" authenticity?

---

### Audience 5: Personal Brand / Thought Leadership

**Who:** LinkedIn network, conference attendees, hiring managers (future optionality), tech media  
**Goal:** Position Jason as a credible voice in federal AI modernization  
**Challenge:** Authenticity; avoiding self-promotion cringe; standing out  
**Success Metric:** Speaking invitations; LinkedIn engagement; recruiter interest; peer recognition

**Questions for the Panel:**
- What's the authentic narrative arc? (Building in public? Federal AI pioneer? Skills-First evangelist?)
- What content cadence works for thought leadership without burnout?
- How do we balance company attribution vs. personal brand?
- What conferences/podcasts should be targeted?
- How do we handle the "I built this at my job" dynamic?
